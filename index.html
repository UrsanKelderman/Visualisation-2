How has AFL Changed over time?

The Australian Football League (AFL) has undergone significant development and evolution over the years. Originally founded as the Victorian Football League (VFL) in 1897, the league started with just eight teams from Victoria. Over time, it expanded its reach and popularity, gradually incorporating teams from other states to become a truly national competition. Key milestones include the inclusion of teams from South Australia and Western Australia in the 1980s, leading to the AFL as we know it today.

Grand Final Outcomes

Throughout an AFL season a team`s primary goal is to win the grand final (GF) and lift the premiership trophy. This is a monumental achievement not only for the current team and players but for the club as a whole.
